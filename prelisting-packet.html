Market Reality
Why Homes Don't Sell

In any given month, homes are selling in Hampton Roads — even in a "slow" market. If a home doesn't sell, one or more of these three things is the reason:

01
Price

The market is brutally efficient. Buyers compare your home to everything else at that price. If you're overpriced relative to competition, buyers move on — and your home sits. Days on market accumulate. Buyers start wondering what's wrong with it. Price is either your biggest asset or your biggest liability.

02
Condition & Presentation

Buyers decide in the first 30 seconds of photo scrolling whether to schedule a showing. Once inside, they decide in 60 seconds whether they're serious. Clutter, deferred maintenance, bad photos, and poor staging kill deals before they start. This is fixable — often at low or no cost.

03
Marketing & Exposure

Posting on the MLS is the floor, not the ceiling. If your last agent stuck a sign in the yard and waited, you got exposure only to buyers already looking. You didn't reach buyers who didn't know they were looking — or agents already working motivated buyers in your price range. There's a difference between being listed and being marketed.

What We Do Differently

Before we list, I give you an honest assessment of all three factors. If there's a problem, I'll tell you — and I'll tell you what it will take to fix it.

The 14-day rule: if your home hasn't generated meaningful activity in the first two weeks, something needs to change — and we'll know exactly what to adjust.
