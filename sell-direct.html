Transparency
How I Price a Direct Offer

Most cash buyers won't tell you how they arrived at their number. I will. Not because I'm required to — because an informed seller makes a better decision, and better decisions lead to cleaner transactions.

Here's the framework I use:

1
After Repair Value (ARV)

I start with what your property would sell for on the open market in fully repaired, market-ready condition. This is based on recent comparable sales in your specific neighborhood — not Zillow, not averages.

2
Cost of Repairs

I walk the property and estimate what it will actually take to bring it to market-ready condition. I'm not padding this number — I do the work myself and know what things cost in Hampton Roads.

3
Holding & Transaction Costs

Financing, insurance, taxes, utilities, and closing costs while I own and renovate the property. These are real costs that any buyer — including retail buyers — ultimately prices into their offer.

4
My Offer

What's left after those deductions is the range I can work within. I'm not hiding a margin — I need one to make the deal work. What I can tell you is that I'm not a wholesaler flipping your contract for a fee. I'm the actual buyer.

The Honest Version

A direct purchase offer will be lower than a retail sale price. That gap is the cost of speed, certainty, and convenience. Whether that trade-off makes sense for you depends on your situation — and that's exactly what we'll figure out together before you decide anything.

Process
What Happens Next

If a direct purchase makes sense, the process is straightforward. Here's what it looks like from our first conversation to closing day.

Step 01
The Conversation
We talk — 15 to 30 minutes. You tell me about the property and what you need. I ask questions. No commitment, no pressure. I'm figuring out whether a direct purchase is even the right fit before we go any further.
Step 02
Property Walkthrough
I walk the property — usually within 48 hours of our first conversation. I'm assessing condition, estimating repairs, and building my offer. This is quick and non-invasive.
Step 03
Your Options Presentation
I present all three options with real numbers — direct purchase offer, as-is list price estimate, and fix-and-list projection. You compare them side by side and decide what makes sense for you.
Step 04
Contract & Earnest Money
If you choose the direct purchase, we execute a purchase agreement. I put earnest money down immediately. You have a written, binding commitment from day one — not a verbal promise.
Step 05
Due Diligence
Short inspection period — typically 7 to 10 days. I'm not looking for reasons to renegotiate. I'm confirming what I already assessed. If something major surfaces that I didn't account for, we discuss it openly.
Step 06
Closing Day — Your Timeline
We close on the date you chose. You pick the settlement company. You get your money. Done. Typical timeline: 14 to 30 days from contract execution — or longer if you need more time.
Flexible Terms Available

Standard cash close isn't the only option. Depending on your situation, I'm open to seller financing, leaseback arrangements (you sell and stay through your move date), or extended closings. If your situation is unusual, tell me — I've probably structured something like it before.
